The internet has been rife with tales about young speculators making life-changing money on high-risk markets. These traders usually opt to stay anonymous, but one successful crypto investor recently decided to share his story with us for the first time — and it’s a wild ride.

Daniel Maegaard, who is just 30 years old and lives in Brisbane, Australia, made a fortune by hedging bets on cryptocurrencies.

An ordinary student Daniel Maegaard worked at a gas station and was struggling to make ends meet. How did an Australian college student manage to turn $4k into a fortune by speculating on the bitcoin boom?

It began 8 years ago, with an investment of a few thousand dollars. In February of 2013, Maegaard — then a 22-year-old psychology student at Australia’s University of Sunshine Coast — was procrastinating on an assignment and came across a BBC article about Bitcoin.

Daniel Maegaard:

If you are OK with a traditional career path, probably my story is not for you, and that’s great. We are all different personalities and should avoid copying other experiences until we are happy with our life.

On the contrary, I wasn’t that satisfied with the opportunity to work in a large corporate company and become a white-collar. I was a graduate student, and while my groupmates were applying to banks and consulting firms, I felt quite indifferent and merely depressed as I couldn’t see myself working in these businesses.

Still relatively unknown to the general public at the time, the cryptocurrency had recently doubled from $15 to $30. He was certain that the coin’s scarcity would lead to an increase in price. So, he decided to go all-in.

“Everyone thought I was crazy,” he says. “But my philosophy was that this was the time for me to take risks — when I was young and didn’t have responsibilities like a mortgage, kids, and bills to pay.”

Daniel Maegaard:

My first piece of advice is to start now. Don’t hesitate, don’t overthink, just do it. It’s an easy and fun process. And even now thinking about my first investments, I feel enthusiasm and nostalgia. As I started investing as a hobby, I was madly in love with this business. Now I have great expertise. But it would never happen if I didn’t start it as a hobby assuming that it’s easy enough to accomplish.

The gamble didn’t take long to pay off. By the end of 2013, Bitcoin had risen to ~$950 per coin, nearly 10x-ing Maegaard’s investment.

After graduating from college, Maegaard enrolled in law school. But as his holdings ballooned, he began to realize that cryptocurrency — not a 9-to-5 lifestyle — was his true passion.

“My parents just didn’t understand it,” says Maegaard. “They’d read about how crypto was used for money laundering and drugs, and they were worried I was making bad decisions.”

By early 2017, Maegaard’s commitment began to pay off. Largely thanks to a boom, his net worth surpassed $1m for the first time. In May, he cashed out ~$600k and bought a house, then reinvested the other ~$400k back into crypto, anticipating another surge.

“Random people, like my grandma, started calling me and asking me about Bitcoin,” Maegaard recalls. “It was reaching a saturation point, and I knew it was time to exit.”

Just before Bitcoin hit its peak, he cashed out, walking away with ~$10m.

A newly-minted millionaire, Maegaard decided to step away and see the world. For the next 18 months, he lived out of a suitcase, traveling to more than 40 countries.

Why Is Bitcoin's Price Rising? The price changes for Bitcoin reflect investor enthusiasm.

Part of what’s different about bitcoin’s rally in 2021 versus 2017 is that institutional investors are now adopting bitcoin, lending it newfound legitimacy and helping to erase the reputational risk of investing in the cryptocurrency.

“The 2017 rally was largely driven by retail investors, whereas this year we’re seeing a massive influx from corporate entities and institutional money managers,” said Mati Greenspan, portfolio manager and founder of Quantum Economics.

Old-school, billionaire hedge fund managers Stanley Druckenmiller and Paul Tudor Jones now own bitcoin and big fintech players like Square and PayPal are also adding crypto products.
